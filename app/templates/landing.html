Physiology × Section 11: Nervous System ×
explain pain inhibiting mechanism in easy language with flowchart

To understand the body's pain-inhibiting mechanisms, it is helpful to divide them into two main categories: Local (Gate-Control) and Descending (Endogenous) pathways.

1. Local Mechanism: Gate-Control Theory

This mechanism explains how stimulating large-diameter nerve fibers (e.g., via TENS or rubbing a painful area) can reduce pain.

Large fibers (L-fibers): Carry non-painful touch/pressure sensations.
Small fibers (S-fibers): Carry pain signals.
The "Gate": Located in the dorsal horn of the spinal cord (Substantia Gelatinosa/SG neuron).
